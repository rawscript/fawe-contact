About Fawe Tuseme Club

Tuseme — a Swahili word meaning “Let Us Speak Out” — is a student-led empowerment initiative designed to amplify the voices of learners, especially girls, within school communities.

Rooted in participatory theatre and life skills education, Tuseme Clubs equip students with the confidence, leadership, and advocacy tools to identify challenges, speak out, and take action.

Founded on the principles of gender equity, child rights, and inclusive participation, the clubs foster a safe space for learners to express themselves, engage with school leadership, and drive positive change in their academic and social environments.

Why Join the FAWE Tuseme Club?

The FAWE Tuseme Club empowers learners—especially girls—to speak out, lead, and take action on issues affecting their academic and social development. Through participatory theatre, peer mentorship, and leadership training, members gain the confidence and skills to become change-makers in their schools and communities.

  • Democratic Leadership & Public Speaking Opportunities
  • Life Skills & Gender-Responsive Education
  • Peer Mentorship & Academic Support
  • Drama, Poetry, and Creative Expression for Advocacy
  • Community Engagement & Action Planning
  • Safe Space for Dialogue on Rights and Wellbeing
